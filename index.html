Postpartum Doula

I’m so excited that you are considering postpartum support, as I truly believe, ALL women and their families deserve extra nurturing during this period of time. I myself had a wonderful birth doula with my daughter but I did not realize how much I needed a postpartum doula. After going through a very challenging 4th trimester myself, I began learning how to help other women to avoid such a rough transition postpartum.

As a postpartum doula, my services are threefold: Emotional, Practical and Educational.

Emotional Support- listening to and validating your feelings about your birth story and current mixture of postpartum emotions. It can be incredibly beneficial to share your thoughts with an experienced, and non-judgemental professional.

Practical Help- such as light house cleaning (e.g. wiping down counters, loading/unloading laundry and dishes, changing diaper pail, etc), holding baby so you may take a shower or nap, running to the store with you or for you, accompanying you to doctors appointments to help with baby, light meal prep, organizing baby’s room, etc.

Educational Guidance- I have an extensive library of resources, evidence-based articles, and books I have studied and can also lend to you if you like. Working with newborns and small children for the past twenty years, and as a mother myself, I have a good deal of experience using different types of baby equipment (structured carriers, slings, strollers, car seats, breast pumps, cloth diapers, etc). I have four different types of baby wearing devices and would gladly bring them along to teach you the basics and let you try them on before you purchase one yourself.

When should postpartum doula support begin? This depends on many factors. Often times, extended family will help out enthusiastically in the early days, but then once the support falls off around week 3, the new family finds that they could use some added support.

Things to think about- Will you birth at home or at the hospital? Will your partner be taking any time off from work? Does your extended family live nearby? What time of day tends to be the most stressful and hectic for you and your family?

I generally work in 3-5 hour shifts during the day. Depending on your level of support from family and friends, I suggest having a postpartum doula visit anywhere from 1-3 times per week.

Overnight support is also an option. It can be a wonderful way to decrease any possible anxiety, and also to balance out your own sleep needs with the needs of your newborn.

My postpartum rate is on a sliding scale of $25-$30 per hour
